Karma & Rebirth

In the Garuda Purāṇa, Lord Vishnu explains that karma—our actions, thoughts, and intentions—create subtle imprints that shape our journey through saṁsāra, the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth.

According to Hindu philosophy, each soul carries three forms of karma: Sanchita, Prārabdha, and Āgāmi. These karmas act like seeds, silently growing until they bear results—sometimes instantly, often in future births.

After death, the subtle body embarks on a sacred journey. It passes through divine realms where the soul is judged by Chitragupta and Yamraj. According to its karma, the soul may ascend to Svarga, descend to Naraka, or be reborn in a new form.

The Garuda Purāṇa narrates how every soul, after death, replays the events of its life in deep review—judging each action with full awareness. Even a final thought at the moment of death influences the next birth.

However, Hindu dharma is not fatalistic. It gives every being the chance to evolve. Through dharma, seva, bhakti, and mindful living, one can reduce negative karmic debt and move toward mokṣa.

Understanding karma and rebirth helps us lead a life of greater awareness, compassion, and purpose.
