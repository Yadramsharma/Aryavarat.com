Sanatan Dharma: Festivals & Rituals

Ancient traditions continuing since Satya Yuga

Sanatan Dharma, the eternal path, celebrates life through vibrant festivals and soul-purifying rituals. These traditions, many of which trace back to Satya Yuga, uphold the values of devotion, family, nature, and spiritual liberation.

ğŸ•‰ï¸ Core Rituals of Sanatan Dharma

  • Sandhya Vandana: Daily prayer performed at sunrise and sunset invoking peace, purification, and divine consciousness.
  • Yajna (Vedic Fire Ritual): Ancient fire offerings made to deities while chanting Vedic mantras. Yajna purifies the environment and the mind.
  • Puja (Worship): Ritualistic worship of deities involving mantra, aarti, flowers, and offerings to establish divine presence in the home.
  • Japa (Mantra Repetition): Silent or spoken repetition of divine names or mantras to calm the mind and focus on the divine.
  • Vrata (Fasting): Observing fasts on specific days (e.g. Ekadashi, Shivratri) to discipline the senses and enhance spiritual energy.
  • Snana (Holy Bathing): Ritual cleansing in rivers like Ganga, especially during festivals like Kumbh Mela, to purify karma and sins.
  • Pranayama & Dhyana: Breath control and meditation techniques from Satya Yuga to awaken higher consciousness.
  • Samskaras: Sixteen life rituals (e.g. Namkaran, Upanayan, Vivah, Antyeshti) guiding every stage of human life with sacred intent.
